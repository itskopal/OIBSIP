MS Dhoni

Mahendra Singh Dhoni (born 7 July 1981), commonly known as MS Dhoni, is a former Indian cricketer and captain of the Indian national team in limited-overs formats from 2007 to 2017 and in Test cricket from 2008 to 2014, who plays as a Wicket-keeper-Batsman. He is also the current captain of Chennai Super Kings in the Indian Premier League.

About

Mahendra Singh Dhoni

Cricketer

He is the first cricketer to be a winning captain of all three major ICC trophies, as under his captaincy, India won the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2011 Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy, the most by any captain. He also led India to victory in the 2010 and 2016 Asia Cup. Additionally, under his leadership, India won the 2010 and 2011 ICC Test Mace and 2013 ICC ODI Championship. He has scored 17,266 runs in International Cricket (including 10,000 plus runs in ODI Internationals). Dhoni is a right-handed wicket-keeper batsman known for his calm captaincy and his ability to finish matches in tight situations for these reasons he is considered one of the best finishers in the game ever, as well as one of the greatest wicket-keepers and captains in the history of cricket.

MS Dhoni is the Legend: ODI’s, T20’s and IPL

In January of 2017, he stepped down as captain of both ODIs and T20Is. As India’s top wicket-keeper in limited overs cricket, he served until the ICC Men’s Cricket World Cup 2019, which marked his 4th and last edition as captain. Dhoni was an integral part of India’s game for nearly 10 years, scoring 4876 runs at 38.09 over 90 tests.
Dhoni, born in Ranchi, made his international debut for India during their Bangladesh tour as the wicket-keeper batsman. In only his fifth ODI against Pakistan at No.3, he scored his first ton with an incredible 148 that set the standard for his relaxed fast batting style which would remain with him for the next decade.
Dhoni’s record-setting unbeaten 183 against Sri Lanka last year further cemented his reputation as a big-hitting wicket-keeper and remains the highest score for an Indian wicket keeper in ODIs. As Dhoni continued to develop his game, becoming an orchestrator in the middle of the order and also leading chases to boost his ODI profile. Last 3rd year he joined just five Indians in reaching 10,000 runs in ODIs
Unfortunately, the last ODI he played ended up being an 18-run World Cup semi-final loss to New Zealand on 10 July 2019.
Dhoni quickly made himself known as one of cricket’s quickest stumpers, boasting his safe method of catching and lightning-fast reaction times. As the third most successful international wicket-keeper (829 dismissals) and only player to record over 100 stumpings in an ODI match, his performance was truly remarkable.
Captaining India, Dhoni enjoyed international success, particularly in limited-overs games. Under his leadership, India reached No.1 on Tests in 2009 – which coincided with his own rise to an enviable position on ODI batting charts. Furthermore, India boasted a remarkable home record; losing only one game since 2006. One of Dhoni’s greatest moments was whitewashing Australia 4-0 in their home opener of 2013 which marked one of his greatest triumphs as captain; that series saw one of his highest scores ever (224).
Dhoni’s innovative captaincy style in T20Is and ODIs was the cornerstone of India’s development into a formidable white-ball powerhouse. Perhaps his most memorable ODI performance came during 2011, in the World Cup final against Sri Lanka where he edged out Yuvraj Singh for an unbeaten 91 and finished the game with an unforgettable six.
Dhoni captained India during 200ODIs and maintained an average win-per-inning of 59.52 while earning his three ICC titles. Other noteworthy victories for him included an historic ODI triseries victory on Australian soil in 2008 as well as Asia Cup victories in 2010 and 2016.
